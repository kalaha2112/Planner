# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A small, close circle — friends and family sharing travel memories and plans. The primary user is also the creator: someone who plans trips, collects memories, and wants to share them with people who know and care about them. Context of use: quiet moments before a trip, looking back after one, or showing the book to someone over coffee.

## Product Purpose

Su Travel Book is a personal travel journal that lives on your phone. It holds your trip cards — past, present, and upcoming — in a sketchbook-style book you open, flip through, and fill with details, photos, and text. Success looks like someone picking it up and feeling the same mix of nostalgia, excitement, and quiet organisation they'd get from their best physical travel journal.

## Brand Personality

Editorial · Intimate · Considered

The app holds the personality of all four physical references at once: Kinfolk's editorial calm and generous whitespace, a Moleskine's worn-in personal quality, Condé Nast Traveller's aspirational confidence, and an architect's sketchbook's sketch-line precision. These are not in tension — they are the same sensibility at different scales.

## Anti-references

- **Generic travel apps**: TripAdvisor, Booking.com, Google Trips — functional but soulless. No destination photography used as decoration, no star ratings, no booking-CTA energy.
- **Millennial pink / Instagram aesthetic**: Soft rounds, watercolour blobs, Pinterest-core pastels. Nothing cute for cuteness's sake.
- The emotional register is aspirational + playful + calm simultaneously — never only one of these.

## Design Principles

1. **Typography carries the memory.** The destination name IS the design on each card. No decorative chrome competes with it. Layout is a composition, not a container.
2. **Personal doesn't mean informal.** Editorial rigour and handmade warmth at equal weight. A precisely placed sketch line reads as intimate, not technical.
3. **Structure enables spontaneity.** The book metaphor — fixed cards, fixed order, page flip — gives the experience rhythm. The sticker and text layer on each card gives it life. Neither cannibalises the other.
4. **Aspire through restraint.** Confidence comes from what is left out. White space is not absence; it is the reason the typography lands.
5. **Share-worthy without performing.** The book must feel beautiful when someone else holds the phone — but must never lose its private, diary-like soul. It is shown, not broadcast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard React Native / Expo accessible defaults. No specific WCAG target or Dynamic Type requirement at this stage.
